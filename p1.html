Main Idea

The objective of my platform is to provide international students with a means to access unbiased information easily and quickly, allowing them to make informed decisions independently. To further aid in this process, the platform gives students the opportunity to seek guidance from individuals who have attended their dream schools. Additionally, students can connect with mentors who share similar interests and hobbies. This platform serves to eliminate information asymmetry, establish a framework for communication between students and mentors, and offer secure payment methods for safe transactions.

My Goal:

Students can be paired with mentors who meet their specific requirements in regards to investment, housing, recreation, academics, and daily life. In addition, news and event updates for their respective regions can also be made available and delivered to them.

Difficulties:

The challenges faced by this website are akin to the classic "chicken and egg" problem, as it requires a critical mass of both mentors and students to attract each other. However, without substantial funding for marketing, it is challenging to generate enough natural traffic. Furthermore, the outbreak of the epidemic has caused many international students to either delay or abandon their plans to study abroad, which has further complicated the promotion of the website.

What made this possible:

Spring Boot, VueJS, Bootstrap

I contributed to the following:

1. Responsive UI

I employed the most up-to-date version of bootstrap (https://getbootstrap.com) to develop my website, ensuring that all pages can adjust to any device used by the client and provide optimal visual displays.

2. Single Page Technology (SPA)

My website also utilizes the single page application (SPA) technology, which allows for the dynamic reloading of content within the current page without having to refresh the entire page. This technology enables only necessary information, such as user input, to be added without repeatedly refreshing unchanged elements of the page, such as the logo, banner, and menu. This approach results in significant savings in website traffic.

3. Auto Complete

To enhance user experience and expedite search results, I integrated autocomplete functionality into the search box.

Nonetheless, there are some challenges associated with the autocomplete feature. If the server responds each time the user inputs a character, it could generate hundreds of unnecessary and resource-intensive requests. To mitigate this issue, I implemented a 100ms delay for requests. In other words, if the user types continuously, the client will refrain from sending requests to the server.

4. Single Sign-On

I developed the single sign-on (SSO) feature for user convenience. Single sign-on (SSO) is a technology that consolidates multiple application login screens into a single page. With SSO, a user can access all of their SaaS applications by entering their login credentials (such as username and password) only once on the SSO page.

5. Pagination

Requesting all data at once would result in a slow initial load, leading to a poor user experience. Conversely, requesting data for only one page at a time would result in a lack of smooth transitions between pages. To address this, I determined a golden ratio, requesting data for 10 pages at a time, allowing users to seamlessly navigate between pages 1-10 while keeping the initial load time reasonable. Once the user reaches the 11th page, another request will be sent.

6. Google Analytic Plug-ins

I signed up for Google Analytics to track user activity and gain insights into the website's performance
