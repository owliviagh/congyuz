The UC ITS Website

UC ITS (the University of California Institute of Transportation Studies) is a group of faculty, research and administrative staff, and students who are committed to advancing the field of transportation engineering, planning, and policy for the benefit of the people of California. It was created by the Legislature in 1947 and has branches at UC Berkeley, UC Davis, UC Irvine, and UCLA.

My contributions:

I assisted in creating the documentation for the UC ITS website, which provides step-by-step instructions on how to create, modify, and add new information to the website.

I created custom CSS codes for several pages of the website. Details are explained and presented in the following sections.

Documentation:

As a member of the team responsible for the UC ITS website, I played a significant role in creating comprehensive documentation that outlines the steps involved in creating, modifying, and adding new information to the website. This documentation covers all aspects of the website, including its design, functionality, and content management system, and serves as a valuable resource for both current and future website administrators. By providing clear and detailed instructions, I helped to ensure that the website is easy to navigate, consistently up-to-date, and capable of effectively communicating important information to stakeholders across the state of California.

Applying Site-wide Custom CSS Codes

The integration of custom CSS is prevalent across the website, drawing primarily from the pre-existing WordPress and Avada frameworks, although I occasionally incorporate my own CSS.

As I utilize the Avada theme pack, virtually all Avada elements have the capacity to be designated a unique CSS name or ID.

• When assigning a class or ID to a particular element, it is important to exercise caution in selecting an appropriate name to ensure that it does not conflict with existing names used by WordPress, Avada, or any additional plugins.

• One potential strategy for assigning class or ID names to elements is to utilize a unique word as a prefix to help ensure that it is distinguishable from other names in use. This can help to minimize the risk of conflicts with names used elsewhere on the website.

Before adding additional custom CSS, it is advisable to first review existing CSS to ensure that modifications can be made without creating conflicts with other custom styles. This may involve examining specific areas where custom CSS has been implemented, in order to determine whether it is possible to make adjustments to existing code rather than introducing new code that may potentially clash with pre-existing styles.
